FAQ

Where do these space gems come from?

Space gems known as extraterrestrial peridots are derived from a very special kind of meteorite called a pallastite. Out of more than 40,000 known meteorites, only about 60 are pallasites.

Pallasites are arguably the most beautiful of meteorites, with a silvery alloy of nickel and iron embaying beautiful, yellow-green, large crystals of olivine. It’s from within these crystals of olivine that gem-quality extraterrestrial peridots are cut. A backlit cross-section of a pallasite meteorite is shown here:

Are these space gems officially recognized?

Yes! The nonprofit Gemological Institute of America (GIA) recognizes and certifies «pallasitic peridot» that is «extraterrestrial in origin». Extraterrestrial peridot is different in composition from any known terrestrial peridot.

Where do pallasite meteorites come from?

Pallasites originally formed near the core-mantle boundary of large asteroids, so their metal comes from the asteroid’s core and their olivine from the asteroid’s mantle. Relatively soon afterwards, geologically speaking, their host asteroid collided with another large asteroid, creating lots of new asteroids. Most of these new asteroids were made of either metal or rock, with only a precious few containing both.

Billions of years later, one of these rare metal-crystal asteroids was struck by a smaller bolide, ejecting small pieces of it to space. A few of these small pieces found their way to Earth, and were either seen fall or, more commonly, found much later. Sometimes they’re found by government-funded search teams such as ANSMET, but more often, by private citizens, and are sold on the open market.

How would I know if they are authentic?

Each piece of jewelry you buy will be accompanied by a certificate of authenticity from the Gemological Institute of America (GIA), an internationally-recognized non-profit gem research institute. The certificate will state that the stone has been analyzed by GIA labs and conclusively identified as a «pallasitic peridot, extraterrestrial in origin».

How old are these space gems?

Older than the age of the Earth! They formed inside large asteroids about 4.57 billion years ago, when the Earth was still a ball of molten rock and iron.

Just how rare are they?

The price of extraterrestrial peridot is set by its true scarcity, as opposed to artificially inflated prices for certain other gemstones.

Why are they better than diamonds?

Extraterrestrial peridots are many times older than diamonds, and formed inside big asteroids before there was even an Earth. Diamonds formed from decomposing organic matter subjected to high temperatures and pressures. Extraterrestrial peridots are better symbols of true eternity than diamonds, which also happen to be thermodynamically unstable and certainly aren’t “forever”. Plus, the supply of extraterrestrial peridot isn’t controlled by sketchy cartels, their prices represent their true rarity, and they’re not associated with armed conflicts and environmental destruction.
